Ancient China

China’s history dates back to the Xia Dynasty (2070–1600 BC), considered the first Chinese dynasty. Early Chinese civilization developed writing, philosophy, and the Great Wall to protect against northern invaders.

The Qin Dynasty

The Qin Dynasty (221–206 BC) unified China under Emperor Qin Shi Huang. He standardized writing, built parts of the Great Wall, and created the famous Terracotta Army to protect him in the afterlife.

Modern China

After the fall of the Qing Dynasty in 1912, China underwent major changes. The People's Republic of China was founded in 1949, blending ancient traditions with modern innovations, making China a global superpower today.

Key Events in Chinese History

  • The construction of the Great Wall of China (7th century BC – 17th century AD)
  • The Qin Dynasty’s unification of China (221 BC)
  • The Silk Road and the spread of Chinese culture (2nd century BC – 14th century AD)
  • The Ming Dynasty (1368–1644) and the construction of the Forbidden City
  • The Chinese Revolution and the founding of the People’s Republic of China (1949)
