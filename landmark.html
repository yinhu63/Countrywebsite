Famous Landmarks of China

Explore some of China’s most iconic landmarks that showcase its rich history and culture.

Great Wall of China: Stretching over 21,000 km, this ancient wall was built to protect China from invasions. It’s one of the most famous symbols of Chinese strength and perseverance.

Terracotta Army: Thousands of life-sized statues of soldiers, horses, and chariots buried with Emperor Qin Shi Huang to protect him in the afterlife. Each figure is unique!

Forbidden City: Located in Beijing, it served as the imperial palace for 24 emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties. It’s a masterpiece of traditional Chinese architecture.

Potala Palace: A majestic palace in Lhasa, Tibet, that was the winter residence of the Dalai Lama. It represents Tibetan Buddhism and traditional Tibetan culture.
