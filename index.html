About China

China is the world’s most populated country, with over 1.4 billion people. It has a rich history and beautiful landscapes, from snowy mountains to tropical forests. Famous landmarks like the Great Wall and the Terracotta Army are in China. The country is also known for its delicious food, such as dumplings, Peking duck, and hot pot. China has greatly influenced art, science, and philosophy and is the birthplace of Confucianism and Taoism.

Giant Pandas

Description: A national treasure protected in Sichuan province, pandas are famous for their black-and-white fur and gentle nature. They are primarily herbivores and spend most of their day eating bamboo, which makes up 99% of their diet.

Location: Sichuan Province

Fun Fact: Pandas can eat up to 38 kilograms of bamboo a day and are excellent climbers despite their size.

Description: The Harbin Ice Festival is held every winter in northern China and features massive ice castles, sculptures, and colorful lights. Artists from all over the world participate in creating these breathtaking structures.
